About our trip

We spent 4 days in Santander to learn how to surf. Every morning we had surf lessons that lasted a few hours, and then we had the afternoon free. We even visited the city of Santender. It was a great experience

Trip Highlights

The Room Distribution

As soon as we arrived, we were hosted in a small villa and the owner was Ale, our rock! Even though we had already chosen our rooms the day before, it wasn't easy for some of us to find them because we had the wrong room numbers; but in the end, we all got settled in properly. Once we were settled, we took a tour of the house and sneaked into each other's rooms.

Surf lessons

"Every morning, we went to surf lessons. First, we chose the right wetsuit size, put it on, and headed to the beach. We had two instructors who were very skilled and friendly. We learned how to stand on the surfboard on the sand, and then we headed into the water. It was difficult and challenging, but in the end, we all managed quite well.

The city of Santander

During a free afternoon, we went to the city of Santander. Right after our surf lessons, we changed quickly and headed toward the ferry that takes you to the city. It took us about twenty minutes to get there, and as soon as we arrived, we were free to explore. The first thing we did was grab some churros; they were delicious! We visited Plaza Porticada and several churches and cathedrals, including the Cathedral of Nuestra Señora de la Asunción and the Parish of Santísimo Cristo. After a couple of hours, we headed back to the meeting point and returned to Somo.

The barbecue with some classmates

One of the most memorable moments of the trip was the class barbecue. What made it so fantastic was the fact that our teacher didn't want us to have one, but we did it anyway without his consent. Some of us had already bought the ingredients, and as soon as we got off the ferry, we did the last of the shopping. Once we reached the hostel, we split up the cooking and table-setting tasks. It was a lot of fun, and we were really proud of ourselves.

Daily Diary

Day 1

We departed on March 8, 2026, from Linate Airport. We landed at 3:00 PM and headed towards Somo. We met Ale, chose our rooms, and he led the way to the beach to watch the sunset. Afterward, we had dinner at the hostel canteen, and finally, we went to bed or stayed up playing games.

Day 2

On our second day here in Somo, we woke up early to have breakfast and then pick out our wetsuit sizes. We were divided into two groups and took turns. Being in the first group, we headed to the beach first, around 8 or 9 AM, for our first surf lesson. We met four instructors who were very clear in their explanations and patient with us. We practiced on the sand before heading into the water. The session lasted a few hours, but time flew by because we were focusing so hard. Once our shift was over, we went back to the hostel to eat, change, and get ready to go out. However, it started raining, so we stayed inside for the rest of the day. Honestly, I think the rest did us some good after such an intense morning!

Day 3

On our third day, since we were in the first group, we were scheduled for the second shift, which meant we could sleep in a little longer. We had something to eat, waited for the first group to finish, and then headed to the beach for our second surf lesson. Once again, we spent a few hours in the water, and I must say we made significant progress in a short amount of time. Many were already getting the hang of it, while others still needed more time, but the first results were already showing. As soon as the lesson ended, we went back to the hostel to eat, change, and get ready to catch the 3:00 PM ferry to Santander. The ride took about twenty minutes. Once we arrived, we were free to wander around the city on our own. The first thing we did was grab some churros—they were delicious! Afterwards, we walked around the nearby area, visiting squares and various cathedrals. Two hours later, we returned to Somo and decided to have a barbecue. Even though our teacher was against the idea, we went ahead with what we really wanted to do: a class BBQ as a memory of the trip. It was so much fun, and it was great to see everyone joining in. After we finished cooking and eating, we cleaned everything up and just enjoyed the rest of the evening.

Day 4

On the fourth day, we unfortunately had our last surf lesson. We were back to the first shift and, as usual, spent quite a bit of time in the water. By this final day, almost everyone had gained confidence with their surfboards, and it was rewarding to see how much progress we had made. We had a blast during our last session on the beach, even though we knew we’d soon have to return to reality. Once our shift ended, we went back to the hostel, ate, and c hanged. We had the afternoon free, so some stayed at the hostel to rest or start packing, while others headed out to the supermarket, went back to the beach to enjoy the ocean view one last time, or took another trip to Santander. Toward the evening, we organized another barbecue, but this time everyone joined in, including our host, Ale. It was a beautiful evening, and I don't think the day could have ended any better. After dinner, we finished packing our bags and made the most of our final night in Somo.

Day 5

On the fifth day—departure day—we woke up very early to catch the bus to the airport. We said our goodbyes to Ale and the hostel and hopped on the bus. As soon as we boarded, everyone fell fast asleep until we arrived. Once we got to the airport, our classmate realized they had left their phone on the bus. There was nothing we could do since the bus had already pulled away. We waited a while for security to open, and then waited for our gate. As soon as it opened, we headed to the plane, and once we took off, we all slept like logs. We arrived in Milan in the afternoon, grabbed our suitcases, and finally headed home.
